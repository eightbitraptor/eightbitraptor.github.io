- I took Friday as holiday from work. Mostly for a bit of pressure relief from
  the relentlessness of the week. I've been meaning to explore NixOS for a
  while and having the day off meant that I didn't feel guilty for sneaking a
  couple of hours to myself while Eddie was napping. So, I switched my Thinkpad
  to NixOS.

  I spent about 30 minutes installing NixOS from the minimal iso, and prompting
  Claude Code to convert my existing Mitamae based configuration management and
  dotfile setup to a nixOS repo, with most of my setup in a flake, and using
  home-manager for my dotfiles.

  And then I spent the rest of my two hour window actually getting the code to
  work on my hardware, reading a lot of manuals and fixing all of the
  clown-shoes code that Claude had generated.

  So now I have a [nixOS powered
  laptop](https://github.com/eightbitraptor/nixos-config) that I can modify and
  update, that mostly looks and feels like the Arch system it replaced. 

  Unfortunately, then the boy woke up, so I haven't had chance to actually
  formulate any coherant thoughts about what I think of it yet.

  Maybe when I buy a phone I can fill the weeknote void with NixOS crap.
